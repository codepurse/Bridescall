18th August 2018

The importance of looking inward instead of outward on your wedding day is an elusive topic when weddings are the subject of conversation. Owing to tradition, people today emphasize the physical aspect of weddings perhaps a tad too much, thereby possibly neglecting the actual experience of having a wedding. Hence, we suggest prioritizing your mental and emotional state to have a more fulfilling wedding experience! Here are tips to prepare for your big day in a meaningful way.

Get to know better your traits that will contribute to a happy and healthy relationship.

Psychology Today says that a good marriage is formed by “cultivating the qualities within you that are necessary in order to respectfully, peacefully, and lovingly live together in a co-creative partnership.” The article further states that such qualities include honesty, faithfulness, and responsibility. Take the time to truly reflect on the qualities and skills you need to improve on in order to sustain a relationship that ensures happiness on yours and your partner’s side.

"Cultivating the qualities within you that are necessary in order to respectfully, peacefully, and lovingly live together in a co-creative partnership."

Get rid of gender roles.

Perhaps one of the most stifling things we fail to notice in our relationship is the expectation we have on our partner by virtue of their being a man or a woman. This is not to say it’s completely an individual’s fault for holding onto this certain belief because—honestly—this way of thinking is so deeply ingrained in us that we have been passing it on from one generation to another. The best we can do before saying “I do” is to examine what expectations we have of our partner that are healthy and realistic, and then get rid of those which will serve neither our partner nor the marriage..

Get ready for the downward slope post-ceremony.

The Knot says it best: “Recognize that your wedding isn’t the same as your marriage.” We often conflate the two, thinking that a terrific, flawless wedding ceremony will lead to a terrific, flawless marriage. “All the stress of planning and coordinating suddenly gets replaced with an unexpectedly sad feeling once the wedding is over,” The Knot says, and it further notes that after all has been said and done, the high of the celebration is replaced with a feeling of what now? This often holds true for anyone who anticipates a huge event, experiences said event, and completes said event (think Christmas season). And this becomes especially harmful to the context of a wedding. Remember: a good wedding will bear no fruit if a good marriage is not the objective.

"Recognize that your wedding isn’t the same as your marriage.” We often conflate the two, thinking that a terrific, flawless wedding ceremony will lead to a terrific, flawless marriage. "
